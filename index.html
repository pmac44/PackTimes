Advanced Estimation

Enter rides you've actually done, ridden the way you'd ride the event you're planning — steady all-day endurance pace, long enough to be honest. Don't use sprints or segment PBs ridden flat out; a short maximum effort will make every plan optimistic. Each sample should be mostly one surface. A section of a longer ride or a Strava segment works well — shorter sections make a single surface more likely. Set each sample's section shape: rolling/loop for sections finishing near their start height, or mostly-climbing for climb segments. Avoid mostly-downhill sections — they measure braking, not fitness. For time, use elapsed time minus deliberate breaks (meals, sleep, long resupply stops) — keep the short stops in. Layers, snacks, bottles, gates: that routine faff is part of real bikepacking, and counting it here is what makes your ETAs match when you actually arrive. Strava's "moving time" hides those stops — if that's all you have, add roughly 10–15% on top for a typical all-day ride, less if you barely stop. Rides you record in PackTimes offer themselves as samples when you save them, with this rule applied automatically: stops under 15 minutes count as ride time, longer breaks are excluded.
